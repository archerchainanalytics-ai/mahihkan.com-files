On the Record — What This Is

This is not a chatbot that answers your business questions. It is not a legal AI service that generates disclaimers and calls them advice. It is not a generic incorporation wizard that outputs the same boilerplate regardless of your situation. It is a structured, jurisdiction-specific compliance guide for digital creators registering a business in North America — built from actual regulatory requirements, actual filing procedures, and actual compliance obligations. It walks you through every step in the correct order, flags what is required versus optional in your jurisdiction, and tells you exactly what to file, where to file it, and in what sequence. The guide is the product. The accuracy is the value.

Origin

Why This Exists

Digital creators — developers, designers, writers, analysts, content producers — generate real income and face real legal and tax obligations. Most of them start without registering a business because the process looks complicated, the information online is contradictory, and professional legal and accounting advice costs more than most creators make in their first year.

The result is a population of people running unregistered businesses, missing deductions they are legally entitled to, operating without liability protection, and accumulating compliance problems that compound over time. The information to do this correctly is public. It is just scattered, jurisdiction-specific, and written in language designed for people who already know what they are doing.

This app was built to change that. One linear guide. Your jurisdiction. Every step in order. No upsell. No subscription. No "consult a professional" as the only answer.

What The App Covers

Registration Workflow

    Business Structure Selection

  • 02
    Name Registration

  • 03
    Tax Registration
    CRA Business Number (Canada) · EIN (USA). GST/HST registration thresholds and voluntary registration. Sales tax nexus basics.
  • 04

    Business account requirements. Separation of personal and business funds. Payment processor compliance.

  • 06

    Platform income reporting, international client invoicing, digital product taxation, IP ownership in a business structure.
